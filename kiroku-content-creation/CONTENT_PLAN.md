## Priority: In progress

### Blog post — When an archivist asks about handwriting AI
**File:** `drafts/museum-archivist-handwriting-ai.md`
**Status:** First draft in progress
**Summary:** A narrative account that begins at a 2019 event at the Discovery Museum, traces the development of the Tyne Built Ships project and its National Archives funding, and arrives at a present-day question from a museum archivist about handwriting recognition. The post argues that AI is a useful tool for archives, not an authoritative one — and that archivists become more visible, not less necessary, in that context.
**Target audience:** Archivists, digital heritage practitioners, people curious about AI in cultural institutions
**Tone:** Reflective, specific, practitioner-facing
